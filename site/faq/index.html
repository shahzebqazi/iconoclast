FAQ

Mastering for heavy music—questions we get before the first bounce.

Questions

Do you only work on metal?
The room is built around metal and aggressive music—that is where the ear is calibrated longest. Adjacent loud genres are fair game if the material fits the room’s strengths.
What should I send for mastering?
Stereo mix at the highest practical bit depth, without clipping the master bus. Leave headroom; we will gain-stage. If you printed hot “for loudness,” say so—we will work with reality, not mythology.
Stems or stereo?
Stereo first. Stems when we agree the song needs it—priced and scoped separately so we never half-remix a mix by accident.
How loud will my master be?
Loud enough for your genre and platform targets, without erasing punch and motion. If you want a brick wall with no transient left, we can talk—but you will hear the tradeoff before it ships.
How fast is turnaround?
Depends on queue and complexity. Ask in email with a real deadline and we will tell you if it is possible without ruining the work.
Revisions?
One round of meaningful tweaks is normal when we missed the mark. Vague “make it louder” loops without a reference are not a revision strategy.
