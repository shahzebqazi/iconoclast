Project onboarding

General intake checklist—your quote may add or tighten details.

Send the stems clean. Send the refs. Keep project talk in email so nothing gets lost. Below is the usual shape of a stem / multitrack job; stereo-only mastering is shorter, but the format and headroom rules still apply.

1. Scope & deliverables

When the work is stem mixing plus mastering (or another scoped multitrack package), you typically receive a mastered primary at 44.1 kHz WAV, agreed alternate masters (for example TV / instrumental + backing blends, or a vocal-up print), and finalized stem exports (vocals, backing vocals, instrumental, or whatever the contract lists).

2. Technical preparation & delivery

  • Format: 32-bit / 44.1 kHz WAV files unless we agree otherwise.
  • Headroom: Keep the master bus out of the red. For stem prints, peaks often land around −12 dBFS or lower so there is space to process.
  • Naming: Use the naming template we send so the session stays organized.
  • Delivery: Upload everything to the cloud link we provide when it is ready.

3. Creative input

  • Reference tracks: 2–3 commercial songs that show the tonal balance, vocal level, width, or attitude you want.
  • Sonic vision: A short statement on the feel of the track.
  • Rough mix: A current bounce so we hear where the song already lives.

4. Workflow & timeline

Turnaround, revision count, and communication channel are set in your quote or agreement. Email stays the paper trail unless we explicitly move elsewhere.

5. Metadata

When metadata is part of the job, we often route credits and identifiers through MusicBrainz so releases stay consistent in public databases. Plan to send final songwriter credits, performer names, and any ISRC or UPC you already have.
