About

What the practice is, what it covers, and how decisions get made.

Practice

Iconoclast Audio is an audio engineering practice focused on heavy and extreme music: production through mastering, with Extreme Metal Mastering as the main workload.

Work is iterative—multiple passes, level-matched checks, and masters that are meant to hold up on more than one playback path. The job is to finish the record, not to run it through a fixed template.

Scope

  • production
  • session bass
  • session guitar
  • mixing
  • mastering

Stereo mastering and stem-based work when the mix needs targeted fixes. Mix and session roles are available when the project calls for them; rates and turnaround live on the Rates page.

Signal chains and processing choices are chosen per project. Expect direct notes when something in the source is limiting the master.

Stance

No borrowed “analog warmth” marketing, no pretend mystique about gear. Preference is for clear intent, honest limits, and sound that still reads after limiting—not a polished generic sheen.

Who runs this

The practice is operated from Toronto by a mastering engineer who also writes software, DSP tooling, and static web infrastructure in support of audio work. Projects are handled with the same discipline applied to engineering: explicit briefs, level-matched listening, and plain-language feedback when a source limit affects the master.

Public channels, examples, and booking routes are listed on Links. Common questions live on FAQ; pricing and scheduling on Rates.

Iconoclast

Iconoclast (noun): a person who attacks established beliefs, customs, or institutions; historically, someone who opposed the veneration of religious images. Here it means skepticism toward audio clichés and template mastering—not hostility toward artists.
